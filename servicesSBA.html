What is the SBA 8(a) program?

The SBA 8(a) Program is a 9 year small business development program. Small businesses who qualify as both economically and socially disadvantage may apply for the Small Business Administration (SBA) 8(a) Certification. This 8(a) Certification should be pursued at the "right time" as it is only valid for 9 years. Additionally the SBA 8(a) Certification is a one-time certification and cannot be applied for again, even under a different business name or different industry. Companies who apply for the certification must meet SBA requirements related to finances (economically disadvantaged) and also prove their social disadvantage.  

The SBA 8(a) program can be a godsend for small businesses who qualify. Not only does it put you in a small competition pool of those who qualify for set asides, but also provides networking assisstance in recieving sole-source (non competetively awarded) and joint venture (via mentor protégé progran) solicitations. In addition to all that, those who are certified get access to a free Business Opportunity Specialist to help navigate federal contracting and management and technical assistance.
